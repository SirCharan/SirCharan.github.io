3. How to maximize chance of allotment in IPO?

Jan 3 2021

Did you apply to a few successful IPOs and they had high listing gains, but you didnt get alloted? Want to increase your chances of getting alloted?

A simple thing is to apply through multiple accounts, maybe from your family members or friends.

However I have a great simple trick which can ensure you almost 100% chances of allotment!

But for that, you will need to first understand the process of allotment which I’ve already covered in the previous article here. Please read it before proceeding.

Lets clear some Myths regarding IPO allotment first:

  1. The time of applying DOES NOT MATTER! Even if you apply on the first day or last day, your chances are the same! The allotment is done through a simple randomized lottery system.

  2. If the IPO is already Over-Subscribed on first day only, then DO NOT bid for more than 1 lot. You won’t be alloted more than 1 lot!

Reasons for all of this have been covered in the previous article here.

So to get a 100% chance to get an IPO allotment:#

Apply as a HNI! If you are fully sure that an IPO is going to be successful, you can apply as an HNI. An HNI needs to apply for a minumum amount of Rs. 2 lakh. This is usually 15 lots.

As told before, the process of allotment of HNI is not by a lottery basis, but rather by a proportionate basis. Which means that no matter what, you will always be alloted the IPO!

Assume you applied for 20 lots, and the IPO gets over-subscribed by 10 times, you will still be alloted 20/10=2 lots!!!

1. Is it risky? What if the IPO fails?

Yes, there is always risk in life. Nothing is 100% sure. There is always some risk in everything you do.

However, to minimize your risk, you can apply for the IPO AFTER it gets over-subscribed on its first day!

If an IPO already got oversubscribed in its first day, (recent example: Burger King & Mrs. Bector), then its going to surely going to give you a listing gain!

2. But what if still something really really negative to company happens or the Nifty crashes?

Well, like I said before, there’s no surity in things. Anything can happen.

So that’s why, to protect the HNIs, there is an option to withdraw your bid ANY DAY before the allotment! This option is not available to the retail investor.

So if a IPO company fails, retail investors will lose, while the HNIs can still exit till the last day.

You can also keep an eye on the Grey Maket Premium to get a good idea of the listing price.(What is Grey Market Premium and how can it give an idea of listing price? )

3. Do I need to be registered with SEBI to be an HNI? Do I need to do anything to become an HNI?

No, you do not need to be registered with anyone, not even SEBI.

4. My Zerodha isnt allowing me to buy more than 15 lots?

You can apply for an IPO as an HNI through the net banking ASBA service provided by your bank. You can read Zerodha’s article on how to apply as an HNI here.

5. But I dont have much money?

If we take the case that you bid for 20 lots(around Rs. 3 lakh), and the IPO was over subscribed 10 times, you will have to pay for only 20/10=2 lots (Rs. 30k) that are going to be alloted to you.

The remaing amount is going to be returned to you (usually it takes less than 2 weeks).

If you don’t have the capital of 20 lots at the beginning, you can apply for something which many investors do, which is called “Margin Funding”.

Margin Funding is basically a loan given to you, specifically for applying for multiple lots in an IPO as an HNI. And it has minimal interest rates, and is a loan only for 2 weeks for the specific IPO period. You can read more here.
