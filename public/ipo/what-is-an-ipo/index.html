What is an IPO?#

An IPO is an initial public offering, or the first time a company makes shares available to the public for purchase. Through an IPO, the company gets listed on the stock exchange for the first time. Most of the companies you see on the exchange today have gotten listed thru IPOs!

You can simply understand an IPO by assuming that the company is just selling it shares, but you’re getting before everyone else, and on prices fixed by the company itself.

Why does a company offer an IPO?#

A company can offer an IPO for various reasons, some common ones are:

Raising Money

Most of the companies offer IPOs to raise money. The money can be used for the purpose of expansion of its current business, paying off debt, or to launch new products or services

Allowing early stage investors to exit

Venture capitalists and Angel investors are the people who invest in the comapny at an early stage. They can look to sell their stake in the company and exit the company if they wish to do so.

Generating Public Awareness

IPOs generate a lot of buzz and activity, and after an IPO, a company comes under the permanent eye of the public and gets listed on the stock exchange. This offers increased exposure and publicity to the company

How does a company offer an IPO?#

Well this is a very long and technical answer, so to simplify I will just cover the important points. The process of filing an IPO involves:

1. Selecting an Investment Bank

A company hires an Investment Bank to handle the process of filing an IPO. It must disclose all its financial records, future plans and all other details, along with the initial price per share. All this information will be tallied by the Investment Bank and this information will be made available to the public.

2. Red Herring Prospectus

Red Herring Prospectus is an important document which a company needs to submit for the registration process which contains all the details about the company. It contains information such as:

  1. Business Model and Details
  2. Strategies and Future plans
  3. Information about the promotors and management
  4. Complete Financial Details
  5. Growth Oppourtunities
