6. How does a company Offer an IPO?

Jan 3 2021

What is the IPO procedure?#

Well this is a very long and complicated process, and it involves a lot of technicalities, so to simplify I will just cover the important points.

The process of filing an IPO involves:

1. Selecting an Investment Bank#

A company hires an Investment Bank to handle the process of filing an IPO. It must disclose all its financial records, future plans and all other details, along with the initial price per share. All this information will be tallied by the Investment Bank and this information will be made available to the public. The Investment is said to be the underwriter in this case.

What is an underwriter?

An underwriter is the party(Investment Banks) which have the job of evaulating the risks of another party (the company). The underwriter’s job is to study the company’s current financial situation, work on their assets and liabilities and then cater to their finanical needs.

The under-writers will assure on the capital they will raise, it is a kind of an insurance or a security for the company. If the IPO fails, the Investment Banks will have to shoulder some of the risks. The company will want this Investment Bank to sell their shares to as many instituional investors and bank to raise money.

2. Due Dilligence#

The due-dilligence require a ton of paperwork and a lot of time and effort to complete the documents required to be submitted to the SEC(Securities and Exchange Commision of India).

It involves submitting documents like:

  1. Letter of Intent
  2. Engagement Letter
  3. Best Efforts Agreement

And many more technical things which we can skip, since they don’t concern us.

What is the meaning of Due Dilligence?

Due Dilligence is a comprehensive appraisal of a business undertaken by a prospective buyer, especially to establish its assets and liabilities and evaluate its commercial potential.

3. Red Herring Prospectus#

Red Herring Prospectus is an important document which a company needs to submit for the registration process which contains all the details about the company. It is a part of the Due Dilligence process.

It contains information about the company such as:

  1. Business Model and Details
  2. Strategies and Future plans
  3. Information about the promotors and management
  4. Complete Financial Details
  5. Growth Oppourtunities

The Red Herring Prospectus of the company is available on SEBI’s official website(sebi.gov.in).

3. The IPO Roadshow#

This is the fun part! After a lot of paperwork and boring work, the company and its underwriters travel door-to-door to various institutional investors to present their IPO.

They market the shares to investors to see the demand for their share and looking at this investor interest, the underwriter can better estimate the number of shares to offer.

4. Setting the IPO price#

After being approved by the SEC, the underwriter and company can decide the effective date, number of shares and the initial offer price. Some of the factors to keep in mind while pricing are:

  1. Value & Reputation of the company
  2. Success/failure of the IPO roadshow
  3. The company money needs
  4. The condition of the economy.

IPOs are usually underpriced, which motivates people to purchase more, and it gets over-subscribed.

5. Going Public!#

On the agreed date, the shares will be listed in the stock market! The underwriter will release the initial shares to the market.

After this, for the first 25 days, the stock price will experience a lot of volatility and the underwriter may step in to sell their shares at higher or lower price to curb market demand.

After these initial fluctuations, a period will start known as the “Queit Perid”. In this period, the promotors and insitutional investors are not allowed to sell their shares to help the stock price calm down and reach some proper levels and moving averages.
