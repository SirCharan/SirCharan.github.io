LinkedIn Social Selling Index

Sep 16 2020

What Is The LinkedIn Social Selling Index?#

LinkedIn Social Selling Index is an index that measures an individual’s extent of personal branding and social influence on LinkedIn.

LinkedIn itself describes the Social Selling Index as

first-of-its-kind measure of a companyâ€™s or individualâ€™s adaptation of the four pillars of selling on LinkedIn, based on a scale of 0 to 100.

The score of 100 is a combination of 4 pillars which each contribute 25 points each, namely:

  1. Create a Professional Brand
  2. Find the Right People
  3. Engage with Insights
  4. Build Strong Relationships

Your score out of 100 is compared with other people in your industry, as well as with your network (1st-degree connections), and calculates the score on a percentage basis.



For example, my LinkedIn SSI is 68% which puts me in the top 2% of my industry (Marketing & Advertising) and top 6% of my network (which is my first-degree connections).

Check out your LinkedIn Social Selling Index here.



It also shows the change in your score every week, as well as compares you with the average score in your industry, as well as in your network.

Why is it Important?#

LinkedIn (and other social media platforms) earn money by showing sponsored posts and advertisements to people. So its main target is to have people stay on the platform for the maximum amount of time possible so that it can generate more ad revenue.

So for that, it rewards good content creators, with high Social Selling Indexes who are people’s favourites by increasing their reach on the platform and giving them more followers and post views, so that they are able to attract people to stay on the platform.

According to statistics, people having high Social Selling Indexes also gain more profile views, have more search appearances and thus are able to gain valuable connections and even get better career opportunities.

How Do I improve My SSI?#

Now comes the question on what each of the 4 pillars are and how to improve them. So lets go one by one

1. Create a Professional Brand#

Under this metric, LinkedIn measures the extent to which you have created your professional brand and includes:

  • Have you put together a complete and professional looking profile?
  • Are you consistently creating content on the platform?
  • Are you getting enough likes, comments and profile views through your posts?
  • Consistently creating content on the platform?

Perks: People who have a higher score than average for this metric, get 35% more profile views and search appearances!

2. Find the Right People#

LinkedIn wants you here to increase the number of valuable connections by adding more people from your extended circles and other leading people from your industry. There are many ways of finding valuable connections:

  • Using the LinkedIn’ powerful search functions along with many filter options
  • Checking out “People You May Know” and “Industry Leaders” and connecting with them
  • Checking out people who may have viewed your profile earlier or liked or commented on your posts, since these people are more likely to connect with you.
  • Using the LinkedIn Lead Builder (paid)

Psst: Always try sending a personalized note along with your connection request.

Even something like a “Hey, Mr, XYZ let’s connect!” would make your request stand out from 100 others.

3. Engage With Insights#

LinkedIn here measures the amount of engagement you’re creating with your posts, and indirectly measures the quality of your posts, and your influence. Some of the measurable quantities are:

  • Number of likes, comments and reshares your posts are receiving
  • Your response rate to your Inmail messages

Perks: Having a good score here would help your posts get 74% more views!

4. Build Strong Relationships#

LinkedIn here wants you to expand your network by connecting with people and further expanding your network by connecting with their connections too.

Psst: One big thing LinkedIn does measure is the acceptance rate to your sent connection requests!

So don’t send connection requests in bulk and send only to people who are more likely to accept them and add a personalized note too
