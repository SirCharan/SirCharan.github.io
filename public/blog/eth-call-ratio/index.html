Ethereum Option's Trade with 90% Probability: Call Ratio Spread+ Call Calendar

In the world of options trading, traders use various strategies to make the most of market opportunities. One such strategy is the call ratio spread, which involves buying one call option and selling two or more call options at a higher strike price. In this article, we will discuss a call ratio spread in Ethereum, specifically buying one 1600CE and selling two 2000CE in the 26th May expiry, while reducing the downside loss by selling a 100 points out-of-the-money (OTM) weekly call calendar.

First, let’s break down the components of this trade. The 1600CE and 2000CE are Ethereum call options with a strike price of 1600 and 2000 respectively, expiring on May 26th. The premium for the 1600CE is $180, while the 2000CE is priced at $60. This means that buying the 1600CE option costs more than selling the 2000CE option, resulting in a net debit for the trader.

To offset the downside risk, the trader plans to sell an OTM weekly call calendar. This involves selling a call option with a strike price 100 points above the current Ethereum price and buying a call option with the same expiration date and a strike price 100 points higher than the sold call option. The weekly options are priced at $8-10.

So, how does this strategy work? The trader’s goal is to profit from a moderate increase in Ethereum’s price while minimizing downside risk. By buying one 1600CE and selling two 2000CE options, the trader benefits from the price increase of Ethereum, but limits their loss in case the price falls. The trader collects a net premium of $60 ($180-$120) by selling two 2000CE options, which helps offset the cost of buying the 1600CE option.

The selling of the weekly call calendar further reduces the trader’s downside risk. If Ethereum’s price remains stagnant or falls, the trader profits from the premium collected by selling the weekly call options. If the price rises, the trader limits their loss since they have already sold two 2000CE options.

One potential downside of this strategy is that the trader’s profit potential is limited. If Ethereum’s price increases significantly, the trader’s profit from the 1600CE option may not cover the losses from the two 2000CE options. However, the risk-reward balance of this trade is still favorable, given the trader’s ability to limit losses.

In conclusion, a call ratio spread can be a useful strategy for traders looking to benefit from a moderate increase in Ethereum’s price while minimizing downside risk. By buying one 1600CE option and selling two 2000CE options, the trader limits their downside loss while collecting a net premium. Adding a weekly call calendar further reduces downside risk and can increase profitability in a stagnant market. As with any trading strategy, it’s essential to carefully consider the risks and rewards before making a trade.
