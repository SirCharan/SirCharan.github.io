What is an IPO?#

An IPO is an Initial Public Offering, or the first time a company makes shares available to the public for purchase. Through an IPO, the company gets listed on the Stock Exchange for the first time. Most of the companies you see on the exchange today have gotten listed thru IPOs!

To make you understand simply, when you buy shares of a company like Reliance Industries, you’re buying shares from people who already have them and are selling to you, because the company was already listed and people have been already buying and selling in it.

But when a new company wants to get listed, it has to allot its shares to a preliminary set of people, from whom the others can buy from.

That is the concept of IPO.

Why does a company offer an IPO?#

A company can offer an IPO for various reasons, some common ones are:

Raising Money

Most of the companies offer IPOs to raise money. The money can be used for the purpose of expansion of its current business, paying off debt, or to launch new products or services

Allowing early stage investors to exit

Venture capitalists and Angel investors are the people who invest in the comapny at an early stage. They can look to sell their stake in the company and exit the company if they wish to do so.

Generating Public Awareness

IPOs generate a lot of buzz and activity, and after an IPO, a company comes under the permanent eye of the public and gets listed on the stock exchange. This offers increased exposure and publicity to the company

Types of IPOs:#

There are 2 types of IPOs: Book Building IPOs and Fixed Price IPOs. It gets very technical for retail investors and beginners to understand, so I’ll try to explain it in simpler terms.

Fixed Price IPO

This is pretty simple to understand. In this type of IPO, a company decides a single fixed price which it announces beforehand, which is going to be the price of a share in the IPO.

Book Building IPO

A Book Building IPO is the most common type of IPO you see in the market. In this kind of IPO, a company gives a range in which an IPO can be purchased, for example in Burger King, this range was 59-60. Lets take the example of Gland Pharma to understand this case better. Gland Pharma IPO had the price range of Rs. 1490-1500. This means that you can bid for any price between this range. The lower bound of the price(i.e. 1490) is called the floor price, while the upper bound is the cap price. The maximum price range in a book building IPO can be 20%, but its usually much lower than that.
