How to buy Ethereum 15x cheaper with limited loss

An Advantage of the ITM bull call spread is that it typically requires less initial capital than buying the underlying asset outright. In the case of this specific trade, the total premium paid for the bull call spread is $100, which would be the maximum loss. However, the total margin required for the trade would be $100 as well, which means that the return on investment (ROI) would be 100% if the maximum profit of $100 is achieved.

On the other hand, buying Ethereum outright at $1600 per unit would require a total capital investment of $1600. Therefore, the ROI would be lower than 100% if the price of Ethereum increases to $1700 or beyond.

It’s important to note that the potential profit and risk of any trade, including the ITM bull call spread and buying the underlying asset outright, should be carefully considered based on an individual’s financial situation and investment goals.

If Ethereum’s price rises above the higher strike price of 1700 CE, the investor in the bull call spread will make a profit equal to the difference between the two strike prices minus the premium paid. In this case, the profit would be ($1700 - $1500) x 1 - $100 = $100. However, the investor in the Ethereum spot buy would make a profit equal to the difference between the market price at the time of selling and the purchase price, which is $1600. If the market price rises to $1800, the profit would be $200 per unit of Ethereum.

If Ethereum’s price stays between the two strike prices of 1500 CE and 1700 CE, the bull call spread investor would lose the premium paid of $100, but the Ethereum spot buyer would still hold the underlying asset, and the value would stay at $1600 per unit.

If Ethereum’s price falls below the lower strike price of 1500 CE, both investors would lose money. However, the loss for the bull call spread investor would be limited to the premium paid of $100, whereas the Ethereum spot buyer would face the full loss of the market price decline.
