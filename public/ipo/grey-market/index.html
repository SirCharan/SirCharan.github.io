What is the Grey Market & Grey Market Premium?

Dec 30 2020

While talking about IPOs, you may have heard the term Grey Market and Grey Market Premium a lot.

Lets dive deep and understand them in detail.

What is the Grey Market?#

The Grey Market is a parallel market. An IPO Grey Market is one where a company’s shares are bid and offered by traders unofficially before listing.

This can take place before the shares are even issued by the company in the IPO!

Since this is an unofficial market, there are no rules and regulations. Market regulators like SEBI and SEC are not involved in these transactions.

However, they are NOT illegal!

What is the Grey Market Premium(GMP)?#

The Grey Market Premium(GMP) is the amount of money people are willing to pay above the issue price of the IPO in the Grey Market.

For example, Burger King had a Grey Market Premium of Rs. 45 before the listing day. It means that people were willing to pay:

Rs. 105 (Rs. 45(GMP)+60(Issue Price))

to purchase Burger King before its listing. The Grey Market Premiums can also be negative, signalling that the stock is going to list lower than its issue price.

Grey Market Premiums are a great way to gauge the market interest of an IPO and accurately determine the listing price of the shares.

Why do people trade in the Grey Market?#

Well the answer to this pretty simple:

  1. Lets assume a person has been alloted in the IPO. However, he believes that the share price on the day of listing is going to down, so he has an option to sell the shares at the GMP, and look to exit!

OR

  1. A person who has not been alloted the IPO, but still wants to desperately buy it before it lists can also buy it through the Grey Market.

Why do companies allow Grey Market Trading?#

As I said earlier, Grey Markets are not illegal, and they are a great way to judge the market interest of a particular IPO before its listing.

And there’s usually a period of 1 week between allocation of IPO and its listing, so that does allow many transactions to take place.
